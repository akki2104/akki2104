## 🏗️ How I Approach Engineering

### **1. Systems Thinking**
Don't build features; build systems. Every component serves a purpose. When designing the intake pipeline, I thought: *Where are the bottlenecks? What breaks under load? Where does data integrity fail?*

### **2. Measurable Impact**
Every decision has a metric. Slider optimization isn't "nicer"—it's "60% faster." This mindset comes from competitive programming: you solve the problem or you don't. No gray areas.

### **3. Depth Over Breadth**
I'd rather deeply understand **one stack well** (backend, RAG systems, real-time patterns) than lightly know everything. Production reliability comes from depth.

### **4. Shipping Mentality**
Ideas are worthless without execution. Built and deployed features in healthcare, fintech, and competitive environments. I know what "production-ready" means.
